FAQ's


Why Canada

For a variety of reasons, namely –

  • Multi-cultural, inclusive, free and open society that warmly embraces every culture and diversity. Continually voted as one of the best places to live with excellent standard of living and free medical care for their Permanent Residents and Citizens
  • Top Quality education in world class universities and colleges offering internationally recognized degrees, diplomas and certificates in diversified programs, with affordable fees and scholarships
  • Students are eligible to work for 20 hours per week while studying and full time during their vacation
  • After successful completion of courses from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI), students can qualify for 3 years Post Graduate Work Permit (PGWP), which may make them eligible to apply for the Permanent Residency (PR)


Cost

If we earned a dollar for every time we got that question, we would be pretty wealthy by now. Easily, this is the most frequently asked question on our social channels. We also make sure that we answer this in the FAQs section. The short answer is – it depends. We do strive to understand the budget of our clients and recommend the best fit course/program, depending on budget and future aspirations. Our deep network with Canadian DLIs gives us the edge to select the best fit course that may not be easily searchable online.

Immigration

If your course/program is in a Canadian DLI and that particular course qualifies for Post Graduate Work Permit (PGWP) Program, then you will get a work permit, dependent on the duration of your course, and provided you meet all other PGWP criteria https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/study-canada/work/after-graduation/eligibility.html .It is important to remember that not all DLIs are eligible for PGWP, also not all courses from a DLI are eligible for PGWP. For example – Fraser International College offers multiple courses, but only the Associate of Arts degree from that college is eligible for PGWP. It can be complex to navigate this landscape and have proper line of sight on list of eligible courses from Canadian DLIs. Feel free to contact us for a no obligation, free of cost consultancy session on this.
